Welcome !

I am Ida Caspary, a PhD student at Imperial College London. I am part of the Data Learning group at the Imperial Data Science Institute and of the Ada Lovelace Academy. My supervisor is Rossella Arcucci. Currently, I am doing Constellation's Astra Research Fellowship, mentored by Asa Cooper-Stickland, on AI control.


My main research is on technical AI Safety; the focus of the the AI safety research group I set up at Imperial College London is Mechanistic Interpretability. I am also investigating how training decisions after pre-training affect what models reveal about their reasoning and goals. My other research interests include robotics / vision-language-action models, and cryptography, e.g. homomorphic encryption.


I moved to Scotland after high school for my bachelor's in Computer Science. I spent one semester on exchange in Ottawa, Canada. For my dissertation project, I researched optimised legacy system upgrades, supervised by Dr Patrick Maier. I upgraded the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) software system of the wildlife radiation database maintained by the Environmental Radioactivity Laboratory (ERL).


I am also very fascinated by faithfulness in chain-of-thought research. As a MATS project, I developed, mentored by Neel Nanda and in collaboration with Anton Poleatev, a little investigative kit called CoTFaithChecker - work that is still ongoing.
