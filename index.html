Welcome !

I am Ida Caspary, an Imperial College London Master's Student.

My main research is on AI Safety: mechanistic interpretability. This is the focus of my research group at Imperial, which I founded in autumn 2024.

My other research interests include robotics, innovations of applied machine learning, crpytography (particularly homomorphic encryption!).


I moved to Stirling, UK, after high school for my bachelor's in Computer Science. I spent one semester on exchange in Ottawa, Canada. For my dissertation project, I researched optimised legacy system upgrades, supervised by Patrick Maier. I upgraded the wildlife radiation system maintained by the Environmental Radioactivity Laboratory for the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA).

I am now doing my Master's in Applied Computational Science and Engineering, tutored by the wonderful Debbie Pelacani Cruz.
