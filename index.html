Welcome !

I am Ida Caspary, an Imperial College London Master's Student – my podcast: Inside Imperial –

& Researcher at the Technical AI Saftety Impact Research Group Programme, Arcadia Impact (2025).


My main research is on AI Safety: mechanistic interpretability. This is the focus of my research group at Imperial College London.

My other research interests include robotics, innovations of applied machine learning, and crpytography (particularly homomorphic encryption).


I moved to Scotland after high school for my bachelor's in Computer Science. I spent one semester on exchange in Ottawa, Canada. For my dissertation project, I researched optimised legacy system upgrades, supervised by the fantastic Dr Patrick Maier. I upgraded the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) software system of the wildlife radiation database maintained by the Environmental Radioactivity Laboratory (ERL).

I am currently particularly fascinated by faithfulness in chain-of-thought research. As MATS Scholar I developed, mentored by Neel Nanda and in collaboration with Anton Poleatev, a little investigative kit called CoTFaithChecker - work that is still ongoing.
